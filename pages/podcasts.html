Feral Hog Militia

This week we talk to Farmer Gabriel Oak who provides some new insights into the current state of the agriculture industry. We discuss the feral hog epidemic in terms of both its macroimpact on the nation and its local effects on Oak's personal farm. Acknowledging the government responses and technology developed to fend off these invasive animals, we consider the long-term chances of securing ecological stability.


Snake in the grass– Burmese Pythons in the Everlgades!

In this week’s episode, we sit down with Lisa, a park ranger in the Florida Everglades. The focus of this episode is to dive into how Florida is fighting to rid their state of a slithering invasive species– the Burmese python! Lisa informs us of the risk these snakes pose to native wildlife and the roles she takes on as a park ranger to eradicate this invasive species from Florida’s environment. Lisa also explains why it’s best to track the pythons before eliminating them. As always, we wrap up our episode discussing how the average resident or tourist of Florida can step in to make a difference in fighting this invasive species!
